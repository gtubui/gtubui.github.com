Images

October 27th, 2021

On Kalso’s website, the company uses images in a unique way. The website is laid out to be a long vertical scroll page, that draws the user into a virtual experience. The images on this page are placed on a worn, newspaper/old paper-like “backing.” When you scroll through the page, the images slightly rotate outwards when they are within the viewport. Along with these images, the page utilizes animated typography and silhouette graphics.

The page seems to be a timeline of the company. It starts with images of the company’s founder, then moves to her inspiration(footprints), process images(factory and building), first store opening (customer interaction and cleaning), advertisement photos (posters and models), success(TIME Magazine, MET Museum), end of production, revival of production(newspapers, models), further success (advertisement photos), awards, then finally, photos of the product with links to them.
